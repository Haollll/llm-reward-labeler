Swing the pendulum up and keep it balanced upright (pointing straight up) for as long as possible, using as little torque as possible.

Observation layout (3 dims):
- obs[0]: cos(theta) — equals +1 when the pendulum is perfectly upright, -1 when hanging straight down
- obs[1]: sin(theta) — 0 when upright or fully down; its sign tells you which side the pendulum has swung to
- obs[2]: angular velocity of the pendulum (theta_dot)

Action: 1 continuous torque applied at the pivot, in [-2, 2].

Task notes:
- "Upright" corresponds to obs[0] (cos theta) close to +1 and obs[1] (sin theta)
  close to 0. Reward being upright, and penalise the squared angular velocity
  (obs[2]) so the pendulum settles rather than spinning, and the squared torque so
  it uses little energy.
- There is no early termination: every episode runs for a fixed horizon (200
  steps), so the objective is to swing up quickly and then stay balanced upright
  with minimal motion and effort for the rest of the episode.
- The pendulum starts in a random position (often hanging down), so a good policy
  must first build momentum to swing up before stabilising.
