Make the two-legged walker move to the right (positive x) as fast as possible while staying upright and "healthy", with smooth, low-energy actions.

Observation layout (17 dims):
- obs[0]: z-position (height) of the torso
- obs[1]: angle of the torso
- obs[2]: angle of the right thigh joint
- obs[3]: angle of the right leg joint
- obs[4]: angle of the right foot joint
- obs[5]: angle of the left thigh joint
- obs[6]: angle of the left leg joint
- obs[7]: angle of the left foot joint
- obs[8]: forward (x-axis) velocity of the torso — this is ALREADY a velocity
- obs[9]: vertical (z-axis) velocity of the torso
- obs[10]: angular velocity of the torso angle
- obs[11:14]: angular velocities of the right thigh, leg, foot joints
- obs[14:17]: angular velocities of the left thigh, leg, foot joints

Action: 6 joint torques in [-1, 1].

Task notes:
- To reward forward speed use next_obs[8] directly; it is a velocity, so do NOT
  difference it across steps.
- The episode terminates early if the walker becomes unhealthy (torso height or
  body angle out of range); max horizon is 1000 steps. Add a survival/alive bonus
  and reward keeping obs[0] (height) and obs[1] (torso angle) in a healthy upright
  range so the walker does not fall over.
- Penalise large control torques (energy).
