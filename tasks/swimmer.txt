Make the swimmer move to the right (positive x) through the viscous fluid as fast as possible, with smooth, low-energy actuation of its two joints.

Observation layout (8 dims):
- obs[0]: angle of the front tip
- obs[1]: angle of the first rotor (joint between links 1 and 2)
- obs[2]: angle of the second rotor (joint between links 2 and 3)
- obs[3]: forward (x-axis) velocity of the front tip — this is ALREADY a velocity
- obs[4]: lateral (y-axis) velocity of the front tip
- obs[5]: angular velocity of the front tip
- obs[6]: angular velocity of the first rotor
- obs[7]: angular velocity of the second rotor

Action: 2 joint torques in [-1, 1].

Task notes:
- To reward forward speed use next_obs[3] directly; it is a velocity, so do NOT
  difference it across steps.
- The episode always runs for the full horizon (no early termination), so progress
  is driven entirely by sustained forward velocity. Penalise large control torques
  (energy) so the swimmer learns an efficient undulating gait rather than thrashing.
