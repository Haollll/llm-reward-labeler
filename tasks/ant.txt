Make the four-legged ant walk to the right (positive x) as fast as possible while staying upright and "healthy", with smooth, low-energy actions.

Observation layout (27 dims):
- obs[0]: z-position (height) of the torso
- obs[1:5]: torso orientation as a quaternion (w, x, y, z)
- obs[5:13]: the 8 leg joint angles (hip and ankle of each of the 4 legs)
- obs[13]: forward (x-axis) velocity of the torso — this is ALREADY a velocity
- obs[14]: lateral (y-axis) velocity of the torso
- obs[15]: vertical (z-axis) velocity of the torso
- obs[16:19]: angular velocity of the torso (about x, y, z)
- obs[19:27]: the 8 leg joint angular velocities

Action: 8 joint torques in [-1, 1].

Task notes:
- To reward forward speed use next_obs[13] directly; it is a velocity, so do NOT
  difference it across steps.
- The episode terminates early if the ant becomes unhealthy (torso height out of a
  healthy range, e.g. flipped over); max horizon is 1000 steps. Add a survival/alive
  bonus and reward keeping obs[0] (height) in a healthy range so the ant stays on
  its feet.
- Penalise large control torques (energy). Minimise lateral drift (|obs[14]|) so the
  ant tracks a straight forward path.
